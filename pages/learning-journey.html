My Learning Journey

There comes a point when you realise that something has to change. For me, that moment hit hard—I was sick and tired of my current life, and I knew I couldn't keep going the way I was. Something had to give. That realisation threw me headfirst into learning, and I haven't looked back since.

What I'm Learning

My main focus has been front-end development, diving deep into the world of HTML, CSS, a nd JavaScript. However, my curiosity doesn't stop there. In my spare time, I explore other areas of coding—not necessarily in a structured way, but more out of interest, to see what's out there and what excites me.

Challenges & Overcoming Them

Like any journey, this hasn't been smooth sailing. Motivation has been a struggle at times, especially when life throws curveballs. It's hard to push forward when things feel tough, but I've learned that because I'm passionate about what I'm doing, those dips in motivation never last too long.

I've found ways to overcome these obstacles—talking to friends and family, researching better mental health practices, and actively working on my mindset. Learning isn't just about coding for me; it's about growing as a person and developing habits that keep me moving forward.

Milestones & Achievements

One of my biggest achievements so far has been completing the Essential JavaScript Course. This was a huge moment for me—it felt like the first time I had truly learned and applied a "proper" programming language. That feeling of accomplishment was a massive confidence boost, proving to myself that I can do this.

How This Journey Has Changed Me

Beyond technical skills, this journey has unlocked my creativity. I now look forward to challenges instead of dreading them, and for the first time in a while, I'm genuinely excited about what the future might hold.

What's Next?

My ultimate goal is to become a front-end developer. Beyond that… who knows? The possibilities are endless, and thats part of what makes this journey so exciting.

Advice for Others

If there's one thing I've learned, it's this: it's okay to stumble, trip, and even fall. What's not okay is staying down. You don't have to get back up immediately—though it's easier if you do—but you must get up. Keep pushing forward, even when it's hard, because every step you take brings you closer to the future you want.
